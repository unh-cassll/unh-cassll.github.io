A meteorological buoy was instrumented to calculate air-sea fluxes of momentum and sensible & latent heat in real time. It has been lovingly dubbed "IRGIE", shorthand for the IRGASON (infrared gas analyzer + sonic anemometer) that serves as the workhorse for the turbulent velocity, temperature, and humidity measurements required to compute these fluxes.
